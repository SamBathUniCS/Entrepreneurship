# PhotoMe Marketing & Social Media Research

This document outline research conducted to support PhotoMe's marketing strategy. PhotoMe is an event-based photo sharing app targeting both students and business. Therefore, the marketing  The research focuses on platform selection and analytics, growth strategies and content formats which drive the highest engagement among student audiences.

Social media now accounts for a major portion of global digital behaviour, with 5.24 billion users, representing 64% of the world's population (Kemp, 2025). The average person spends 2 hours 24 minutes daily on social platforms (Kemp, 2023) and 76% of all users say social media (ads, influencer posts, brand content, etc.) has influenced some percentage of their purchases over the past six months (Q2 2025 Sprout Pulse Survey). PhotoMe represents both an acquisition channel and a product use case, since users are motivated to share event highlights on social media directly from the app, further developing our marketing strategy. 


## Social Media Platform Analytics

### Engagement Rate Benchmarks by Platform

Engagement rates vary substantially across platforms and these differences have direct implications for where PhotoMe should invest content and marketing effort.

| Platform | Average Engagement Rate | Source | Largest Age Demographic (FSB, 2024)
|---|---|---|---|
| Instagram | 0.43% | Digitaloft (2025) |18-24 (30.8%)|
| Facebook | 0.063% | Digitaloft (2025) |25-34 (29.9%)|
| X (Twitter) | 0.029% | Digitaloft (2025) |25-34 (38.5%)|
| TikTok| 2.65% | Socialinsider (2022)|25-34 (60%)|

Instagram's engagement rate of 0.43% is already low compared to TikTok, meaning any additional sharing barriers, such as photo formatting, significantly decrease the likelihood of users posting event content (Digitaloft, 2025). This reinforces the need for PhotoMe's quick social media sharing feature.

### Short Form Video Dominance

Short form video generates 2.5× more engagement than long form video (Rivera, 2025). This format is at the centre of both TikTok and Instagram Reels and should be the primary content format for PhotoMe's social media output. This is the core reason TikTok, Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts have dominated the digital marketing space (Forbes, 2024). PhotoMe's AI-generated highlight reels are also aligned with this consumption trend, as they produce short form montages and reels automatically from event photos.

## Platform Statistics

### TikTok

- TikTok's user base grew 18.4% from 2024 to 2025, compared to Instagram's 7.2% growth over the same year (SQ Magazine, 2026).
- 18–24 year olds make up 38% of TikTok's total user base, compared to 31% for the same age group on Instagram (SQ Magazine, 2026). This is critically the target demographic for PhotoMe's event attendees.
- Over 50% of Gen Z users use TikTok and Instagram as search engines over Google (House of Marketers, 2026), which highlights how visibility on TikTok directly influences app discoverability among PhotoMe's target demographic.
- TikTok had approximately 1.59 billion monthly active users in 2025 (House of Marketers, 2026).
- The average TikTok user opens the app 19 times per day and spends approximately 58 minutes on the platform daily (House of Marketers, 2026), highlighting the reach PhotoMe's social media campaign could achieve utilising Tiktok as a platform.

Overall, TikTok represents a high priority new platform for student acquisition due to its dominant 18–24 demographic, superior engagement rate and fast growth trajectory over Instagram.

### Instagram

- Instagram has over 2 billion monthly active users globally (Billo, 2026).
- Instagram Reels generate significantly higher reach than static posts or Instagram tories (Socialinsider, 2022).
- The average Instagram engagement rate is 0.43% (Digitaloft, 2025) of which Instagram Reels consistently outperform this benchmark.
- Instagram is still the primary photo sharing platform for 18–24 year olds in the UK, making it essential for PhotoMe's brand presence and marketing.

Instagram should remain the PhotoMe's brand center and is the most suitable platform for diverse visual content such as reels, montages and carousels. However, TikTok should be prioritised for discovery and viral growth.

### Snapchat

- Snapchat reaches 90% of all 13–24 year olds in the US and UK (Snap Inc, 2024).
- Snapchat's Spotlight feed (which uses short-form video discovery) allows organic reach similar to TikTok's For You Page, further solidifying itself as a platform to share PhotoMe's brand content.
- The platform's QR code (Snapcode) infrastructure is culturally established with student users — directly complementing PhotoMe's QR-based event joining mechanism.

> Due to the widespread usage of short form content, reposting the same videos across these three platforms has shown to triple the brand reach, given the low budget of our startup.
