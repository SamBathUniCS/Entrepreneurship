# PhotoMe Marketing & Social Media Research

This document outline research conducted to support PhotoMe's marketing strategy. PhotoMe is an event-based photo sharing app targeting both students and business. The research focuses on platform selection and analytics, growth strategies and content formats which drive the highest engagement among student audiences.

Social media accounts for the majority global digital behaviour, with 5.24 billion users representing 64% of the world's population. The average person spends 2 hours 24 minutes daily on social platforms (Kemp, 2023) and 76% of all users say social media has influenced some percentage of their purchases over the past six months (Q2 2025 Sprout Pulse Survey). PhotoMe represents both an acquisition channel and a product use case, since users are motivated to share event highlights on social media directly from the app, further developing our marketing strategy. 


## Social Media Platform Analytics

### Engagement Rate Benchmarks by Platform

Engagement rates vary substantially across platforms and these differences have direct implications for where PhotoMe should invest content and marketing effort.

| Platform | Average Engagement Rate | Source | Largest Age Demographic (FSB, 2024)
|---|---|---|---|
| Instagram | 0.43% | Digitaloft (2025) |18-24 (30.8%)|
| Facebook | 0.063% | Digitaloft (2025) |25-34 (29.9%)|
| X (Twitter) | 0.029% | Digitaloft (2025) |25-34 (38.5%)|
| TikTok| 2.65% | Socialinsider (2022)|25-34 (60%)|

Instagram's engagement rate of 0.43% is already low compared to TikTok, meaning any additional sharing barriers, such as photo formatting, significantly decrease the likelihood of users posting event content (Digitaloft, 2025). This reinforces the need for PhotoMe's quick social media sharing feature.

### Short Form Video Dominance

Short form video generates 2.5× more engagement than long form video (Rivera, 2025). This format is at the centre of both TikTok and Instagram Reels and should be the primary content format for PhotoMe's social media output. This is the core reason TikTok, Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts have dominated the digital marketing space (Forbes, 2024). PhotoMe's AI-generated highlight reels are also aligned with this consumption trend, as they produce short form montages and reels automatically from event photos.

## Platform Statistics

### TikTok

- TikTok's user base grew 18.4% from 2024 to 2025, compared to Instagram's 7.2% growth over the same year (SQ Magazine, 2026).
- 18–24 year olds make up 38% of TikTok's total user base, compared to 31% for the same age group on Instagram (SQ Magazine, 2026). This is critically the target demographic for PhotoMe's event attendees.
- Over 50% of Gen Z users use TikTok and Instagram as search engines over Google (House of Marketers, 2026), which highlights how visibility on TikTok directly influences app discoverability among PhotoMe's target demographic.
- TikTok had approximately 1.59 billion monthly active users in 2025 (House of Marketers, 2026).
- The average TikTok user opens the app 19 times per day and spends approximately 58 minutes on the platform daily (House of Marketers, 2026), highlighting the reach PhotoMe's social media campaign could achieve utilising Tiktok as a platform.

Overall, TikTok represents a high priority new platform for student acquisition due to its dominant 18–24 demographic, superior engagement rate and fast growth trajectory over Instagram.

### Instagram

- Instagram has over 2 billion monthly active users globally (Billo, 2026).
- Instagram Reels generate significantly higher reach than static posts or Instagram tories (Socialinsider, 2022).
- The average Instagram engagement rate is 0.43% (Digitaloft, 2025) of which Instagram Reels consistently outperform this benchmark.
- Instagram is still the primary photo sharing platform for 18–24 year olds in the UK, making it essential for PhotoMe's brand presence and marketing.

Instagram should remain the PhotoMe's brand center and is the most suitable platform for diverse visual content such as reels, montages and carousels. However, TikTok should be prioritised for discovery and viral growth.

### Snapchat

- Snapchat reaches 90% of all 13–24 year olds in the US and UK (Snap Inc, 2024).
- Snapchat's Spotlight feed (which uses short-form video discovery) allows organic reach similar to TikTok's For You Page, further solidifying itself as a platform to share PhotoMe's brand content.
- The platform's QR code (Snapcode) infrastructure is culturally established with student users — directly complementing PhotoMe's QR-based event joining mechanism.

> Due to the widespread usage of short form content, reposting the same videos across these three platforms has shown to triple the brand reach, given the low budget of our startup.

## Case Study of Fast Growing Photo Apps and Marketing Strategies:

### BeReal

BeReal is the most directly comparable app to PhotoMe with regards to app content,domain, target audience and growth model. BeReal positioned themselves as the anti-social media, prioritising living in the moment and capturing live photos without filters or perfection like Instagram.

- BeReal was launched in 2020 but grew fairly slowly until it started a targeted campus ambassador programme in January 2022 across US and UK universities. This paid college students $30 per referral and $50 for an app download and review. BeReal also hosted parties across college campuses, where admission to the party was free if college students downloaded the app and added five friends.
- With campus rollout, BeReal experienced a 315% year-over-year user growth and in 2022 it finished the year as Apple's iPhone App of the Year (Contrary Research, 2026).
- BeReal quickly reached 28 million daily active users, however, BeReal's collapse was equally rapid. The app lost the majority of its active users within 18 months due to poor retention mechanics, as there were no new features that gave users a reason to return consistently.
- Tiktok introduced similar real-time picture (click a picture in the moment) features, following the rise of BeReal.

**Key Lessons:**
1. Campus ambassador programmes are the most effective zero-cost student acquisition channel, as demonstrated by BeReal's 2022 growth. However, it is noted the startup cost associated with this marketing model
2. Retention mechanics are critical. This supports the need for PhotoMe's upload to unlock model, leaderboards and AI highlight reels, which are are designed specifically to address the retention gap that caused BeReal's decline.
3. PhotoMe should consider adding functionality for real-time/live event photos, following the popularity of in the moment captures with Bereal

### Locket
Locket is a photo-sharing app designed for intimate, real-time connection. It skips public feeds in favor of instant, personal updates that appear immediately upon unlocking the phone. It is designed for closer smaller social circles as it is linked by user phone number than username. Similar to BeReal, the app does not have filters or effects on its camera, intended to make users capture their true selves. 

- The app raised $12.5M in its initial seed round, backed by OpenAI
-  In the first three months since its launch, it saw over 1 billion photos shared and gained more than 20 million downloads.
- Locket's growth was driven almost entirely by strong TikTok content strategy.  10% of Locket's TikTok videos exceed the median view count by 10× (20,000 views) and over 3% achieve viral status at 50× views, collectively generating over 250 million TikTok views (Shortimize, 2026). 
- Locket's retention uses "object permanence" as the widget means a friend's photo is passively present on your phone without requiring you to open an app. This produces significantly stronger retention than BeReal's active daily engagement model. 
- Locket's user base is 70% aged 13–22 globally (The Up and Up, 2025)

**Key Lessons:**
1. TikTok organic growth is more effective and sustainable than broad influencer campaigns. Identifying one highly repeatable, authentic content format and executing at volume drove 250 million views with minimal budget, which PhotoMe could leverage

2. Passive features can drive stronger retention than active daily prompts, Locket's widget model outperforming BeReal's daily notification approach. This is supported by PhotoMe's facial recognition notification system , automatically alerting users when they appear in new event photos (creates a passive reason to return to the app without requiring daily action)

3. Intimate social graphs produce higher retention rates than broad public networks, particularly among the younger demographic. This supports the use of PhotoMe's friends list privacy settings and the value of positioning the app around known social circles (such as university societies and friend groups) as well as open public events.


### Tinder

- Tinder grew to millions of users with campus ambassador campaigns in 2012–2013 (Future.com, 2022).
- The strategy relied on students hosting "Tinder parties" where attendees had to download the app to enter, which is a direct, event-based activation model.
- The event based nature of PhotoMe makes it uniquely suited to a similar activation model. Ambassadors could host society events where PhotoMe is the official photo platform, creating an immediate, tangible use case.

### Spotify

- Spotify's student acquisition strategy relied heavily on institutional partnerships (university email verification for student discounts) combined with event sponsorships.
-  University society partnerships serve a similar role, by embedding PhotoMe into society events as the default photo platform, the app gains recurring institutional use rather than one-time downloads.
