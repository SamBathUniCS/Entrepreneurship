## 6. Facial Recognition & Biometric Data

This section should be read carefully. Facial recognition involves the processing of **special category biometric data** under UK GDPR and carries significant privacy implications.

### 6.1 How It Works

PhotoMe uses facial recognition to identify registered users within photos uploaded to event galleries. When a photo is uploaded, the system compares faces in that photo against the facial embeddings of consenting, registered users. You will receive a notification when photos containing you are identified.

### 6.2 Consent

- Facial recognition is **strictly opt-in**. It is not activated by default.
- You must provide explicit, standalone consent to facial recognition processing during onboarding or at any point in your account settings. This consent is separate from your agreement to these Terms.
- You may withdraw this consent at any time in **Settings → Privacy → Facial Recognition**. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

### 6.3 What We Store

- We store a mathematical **facial embedding** (a numerical representation of facial geometry) — not a copy of your face image — for photo matching.
- Facial embeddings are stored on UK/EU servers (AWS eu-west-2) only.
- We do not build or retain embeddings for individuals who have **not** registered and consented to facial recognition. Unidentified faces in uploaded photos are not stored or processed speculatively.
- Embeddings are deleted within **30 days** of account closure or consent withdrawal.

### 6.4 Privacy Controls

You can configure facial recognition at three levels at any time:

- **All events** — you will be matched and notified across all galleries you are a member of
- **Friends only** — matching is limited to galleries created by users on your friends list
- **Off** — facial recognition is fully disabled; you will not be matched in any gallery

### 6.5 Children

Users aged 13–17 require verifiable parental or guardian consent before facial recognition is activated on their account. Event organisers using the Business tier who host events that may include under-18s are required to obtain appropriate consent from minors or their guardians as a condition of their organiser agreement.
